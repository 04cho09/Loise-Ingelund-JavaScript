American Psycho

Directed by: Marry Harron

4,5

The movie, American psycho follows Patrick Bateman, an investment banker in New York. He battles with his alternate psychopathic ego/identity which hinders him from fitting into the world of the New York elite. During the duration of the movie, we see Bateman and his violent and hedonistic fantasies as he spirals more into his urges and psychopathy.

Rating and why

I would personally not give this movie a 5-star rating as the movie simply does not have a typical structure, whilst it's not bad it was just hard for me to follow it. But the movie is supposed to be confusing as Bateman could be perceived as an unreliable narrator, but it is just was not my style. Since I still give it a 4,5 out of 5, means that I did enjoy it greatly, I'm just not a fan of it's "open ending" meaning movies where the ending isn't much of an ending since it's up to the person watching to imagin what happens next with the set up the movie gives you. But as I've mentioned, I am a big fan of gore and more specifically blood, which this movie features a lot.

The cabin in the woods

Directed by: Drew Goddard

5

The cabin in the woods follows a group of five people, it follows the structure and premise of a classic slasher film as the group of friend were staying in a cabin in the middle of the woods, alone. The group has a stereotypical slasher movie set up with more specifically an athlete, the innocent one, the smart one, the whore and the stoner.

Rating and why

I particularly liked this movie because of how stereotypical it was. It started as kind of dumb and carefree. But later dives into darker themes, with quite gruesome visuals that I enjoyed. I also really liked the structure of the movie as it was very simple and easy to follow. The movie itself does not have any hidden or deeper themes as other movies I'm featuring but I is still one of my favorite horror movies, which is why I rated it a 5 out of 5.

Disclaimers this movie Contains:

Graphic Violence
Gore
Sexual content
